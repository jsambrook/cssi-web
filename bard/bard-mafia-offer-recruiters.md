### Core Argument

LinkedIn recruiters are the most complained-about professionals on the platform. Mass messages, zero personalization, clearly spraying and praying. Nobody trusts them because they're chasing commissions, not genuine fit.

Mafia offer for a recruiter: "We only contact candidates who genuinely match the role. If we contact you and it's not a real fit, we'll send you $50. We place you in a role where you're genuinely excited about the work within 90 days, or we keep recruiting at no charge until we do. We don't get paid unless you've been in the role for six months and you're still happy."

What the recruiter must do to make this work without going broke:

- Stop operating on volume. Work with 50 qualified candidates, not 500 random profiles.
- Deeply understand client companies: culture, real requirements, growth trajectory.
- Screen ruthlessly. Say no to placements that won't stick.
- Measure success by retention and satisfaction, not placements made.

The healthcare application: EvergreenHealth and other health systems can apply the same logic to nursing recruitment. Stop blast-recruiting every RN with a pulse. Target nurses whose skills, schedule preferences, and career goals actually match what the facility needs. Guarantee the fit, or refund the recruiting fee.

### Key Points to Cover

- The core problem: recruiter spam. Zero personalization. Candidates feel like numbers.
- What candidates actually want: to be contacted only when it's genuinely relevant.
- What hiring managers want: candidates who are actually interested and qualified, not a firehose of resumes.
- The unrefusable offer: $50 penalty for bad outreach, 90-day placement guarantee, six-month satisfaction guarantee.
- What the recruiter must do: deep understanding of roles, ruthless screening, willingness to say no, success measured by retention.
- The healthcare staffing angle: EvergreenHealth's nursing recruitment constraint. Same principle applies. Stop filling bodies. Start matching people to roles they'll stay in.
- The payoff: a recruiter who guarantees fit and satisfaction dominates because clients and candidates both trust them.
