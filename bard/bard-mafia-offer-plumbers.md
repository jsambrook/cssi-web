### Purpose

### Core Argument

Homeowners dread plumbers because the bill is always a surprise. You're quoted $300 and end up paying $800 because there's "structural damage" or "unexpected complications." Customers feel squeezed. The core problem is that plumbers do inadequate diagnostics upfront and then pass the cost of discovery to the customer.

Mafia offer: "Fixed-price quotes, in writing, before any work begins. If we find additional issues, we stop work, show you the problem, and get your approval before proceeding. The final bill will never exceed our quote by more than ten percent, or the overage is free."

This forces the plumber to do proper diagnostics upfront, invest in inspection equipment, and train technicians to assess thoroughly before quoting.

### Key Points to Cover

- The core problem: invoice shock. Quoted one price, charged another. "Unexpected complications" that always seem to appear after work has started.
- The customer's emotional experience: feeling trapped once the plumber has already started, unable to say no to the additional cost.
- Why plumbers haven't solved this: they don't invest in thorough upfront diagnostics because it's faster (for them) to discover problems as they go.
- The unrefusable offer: written fixed-price quotes, ten percent cap on overages, or the overage is free.
- What the plumber must do: invest in camera inspections, train technicians to assess thoroughly before quoting, build in reasonable contingencies to their quotes, develop standard pricing for common scenarios.
- The payoff: the plumber who eliminates invoice shock gets all the referrals because homeowners tell their friends "this is the one plumber who won't surprise you."
- The financial reality: thorough diagnostics take more time upfront but reduce callbacks, disputes, and reputation damage.
