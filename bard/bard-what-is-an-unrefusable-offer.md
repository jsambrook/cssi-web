## Blog Article Requirements Document

### Purpose

Introduce the concept of the "mafia offer" (unrefusable offer) to small business owners. Explain that it's not a marketing gimmick or a tagline. It's a structural commitment that requires you to actually solve the core problem your industry creates for customers. If you don't have one, you're competing on price, which is a race to the bottom.

### Core Argument

An unrefusable offer is not a slogan. It's a promise so strong that saying "no" to it requires explanation. But here's the key: you can only make an unrefusable offer if you've actually solved the core problem your industry creates for customers. Every industry has one. Contractors create uncertainty. IT providers create complexity. Insurance agents create distrust. Until you crack that core problem in your business, you don't have an unrefusable offer. You just have nice words on a page.

The second key insight: the business owner who makes an unrefusable offer never has to pay off the guarantee because they've reengineered their business to consistently deliver. The guarantee is proof of competence, not a risk.

### Key Points to Cover

- Definition: An unrefusable offer puts something genuinely attractive in front of the customer, like a guarantee of success, a money-back promise, or better yet, a triple-your-money-back promise.
- Why most businesses don't have one: they haven't solved the core problem their industry creates.
- The core problem concept: every industry has a perception problem with the public. Contractors are unreliable. Plumbers surprise you with costs. SEO people sell rankings nobody cares about. Insurance agents chase commissions.
- The guarantee signals competence: if a contractor offers triple-your-money-back on timeline and budget, customers think "this person is so confident they'll deliver that they're willing to bet their shirt on it."
- The real work: reengineering your business so you never have to pay off the guarantee.
- The competitive advantage: once you've done this work, you own your market. Everyone else is still playing the old game.
- Brief examples across industries (contractors, plumbers, IT, SEO) to show the pattern is universal.
